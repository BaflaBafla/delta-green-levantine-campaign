# Session Zero — The Levantine Engine

Purpose: ensure player comfort, consent, and expectations. Use this handout at least one week before Session 1, or run-at-table at the start of your first meeting.

1) Short description (one paragraph)
- The Levantine Engine is a Delta Green campaign set in the Levant (Israel / Palestine / Syria, 2026). It explores a metaphysical Pact tied to regional history and modern conflict. Play focuses on moral dilemmas, investigation, and survival horror.

2) Content warnings (explicit)
- Depictions of: modern armed conflict, death of civilians, hospital/medical trauma, torture and interrogation, political and religious themes, cultural trauma, forced displacement, mentions of sexual violence (rare), graphic body alteration (mutation), and character death.

3) Lines & Veils (sample defaults — set table rules with players)
- Lines (never present in detail): explicit sexual violence scenes; live-streamed real-world atrocities; players forced to roleplay real-world identity trauma of other players.
- Veils (fade-to-black): severe medical gore, graphic descriptions of corpses, and intimate torture scenes. Handler will summarize rather than describe bodily detail.

4) Safety tools at the table
- X-Card (physical or digital) — any player taps/clicks to stop and reframe a scene.
- Signal words: "Pause" (stop and check in), "Time" (I need 60 seconds), "Skip" (I need to move past a scene)
- Buddy system: each player names a check-in buddy. After heavy scenes, the Handler runs a one-minute check-in.

5) Consent and boundaries
- Each player lists up to three hard limits and three soft limits before Session 1.
- Hard limits are absolute: the Handler will not include those elements. Soft limits may be included with reduced detail or veils.

6) Safety debrief
- After any session with high emotional content, the group does a 10-minute debrief. The Handler will: ask for three words describing how players feel, offer resources (break, water, step outside), and follow up privately within 48 hours.

7) Reskin option
- If any player prefers fictionalized geography and factions, the campaign can be reskinned to use fictional place names and neutral faction labels. This reduces the chance of triggering real-world political trauma while preserving plot mechanics.

8) Confidentiality & Respect
- Players agree not to post session transcripts, player-identifying details, or photos of real player faces without consent. In-game NPCs and events may be discussed with spoilers consented by the table.

9) After the session (handler checklist)
- Run the 10-minute group check-in.
- Note any players who used safety tools and send a private follow-up the next day.
- Keep a short log of particularly heavy scenes and whether the table responded positively to the handling.
