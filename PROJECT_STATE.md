## 1. Cosmology: Three-Layer Truth

### Layer 1 — The Broken Seals (Historical)
In the 1st century CE, a syncretic sect (fringe Judean, Philistine, Hellenistic mystics) recognized that regional egregores — psychic entities feeding on collective human emotion — were destroying humanity. They enacted a massive binding ritual using the geography of the Levant as a cage. Archaeological sites, cave systems, and specific geological formations serve as "seals" that hold the egregores at bay.

Modern bunker-busters, tunnel networks, and artillery are physically destroying these seals.

### Layer 2 — The Blood Engine (Contemporary)
The egregores are not passive prisoners. They feed on a specific blend of collective emotion: territorial terror, righteous fury, generational trauma. The modern conflict is a self-sustaining psychic engine. Cults on all sides covertly steer leadership to ensure the war never stops — because peace would starve the entities.

The horrifying revelation: the conflict is not *failing* to end. It is being *maintained*.

### Layer 3 — The Pact / The Debt (Metaphysical)
The original binding was not a prison — it was a *negotiated contract*. The egregores agreed to be bound in exchange for a steady tithe of psychic energy. The Pact has specific terms: precise rituals, specific sacrifices, geographic boundaries, temporal cycles. It is a cosmic debt instrument.

Modern actors are violating the terms through ignorance or greed. The egregores are waking — not to destroy, but to *renegotiate*. The cults believe they can control the renegotiation. They are wrong.

## 2. The Egregores

### Moloch / Baal-Hammon — "The Consumer of the Future"
- **Domain**: Sacrifice of the next generation for survival of the state
- **Manifestation**: Statistical inevitability of endless youth casualty; fire-scented thermals; heat shimmer over battlefields
- **Cult**: Infected mid-level officers and strategists in IDF and Hamas who optimize operations for maximum generational hatred
- **Pact Term**: "First-born of every generation" — interpreted metaphorically (the young sent to die) but the egregore is pushing for a literal reading

### Dagon — "The Deep Terror of the Coast"
- **Domain**: Claustrophobic pressure of being trapped, blockaded, forced underground
- **Manifestation**: Ancient salt-water smell miles inland; tunnels that shouldn't exist; non-Euclidean subterranean geometry; the whisper of crushing abyssal depths
- **Cult**: "Keepers of the Well" — a network within Gaza tunnels who view Dagon as a psychic sponge absorbing collective trauma
- **Pact Term**: "What lies beneath" — the tunnels themselves are bargaining chips; Dagon wants them extended to the deep places

### The Sicarii Egregore — "The Pure Fire"
- **Domain**: Suicidal martyrdom and apocalypse over compromise
- **Manifestation**: Psychic contagion among extremists; the phantom scent of ancient blood and copper; sica daggers appearing in dreams
- **Cult**: The Order of the Broken Sica (ancient lineage, cross-border, includes Palestinian Christians, Druze, unorthodox Jewish mystics)
- **Pact Term**: "The final sacrifice" — the egregore was bound by a promise that the watchers would never stop watching. It wants release from its vigil.

## 3. Pact Terms Interaction — The Trilemma

The Pact is a single contract with three parties. The terms interlock: fulfilling one egregore's demand automatically advances at least one other's agenda. This is the trap that keeps the Order of the Broken Sica in paralysis — and the engine that forces the agents toward the Fourth Path.

### 3.3 The Strategic Landscape

**No single action is clean.** This drives the campaign's moral horror.

| Action | Pact Δ | Secondary Effect | Who Benefits |
|--------|--------|-----------------|--------------|
| Stop a tunnel operation | +1 (violating Dagon's term) | Fewer young soldiers die | Moloch is starved |
| Allow a tunnel to collapse | –1 (Dagon term fulfilled) | Casualties in the deep | Moloch fed |
| Prevent a settler attack | +1 (violating Sicarii) | De-escalation | All starved |
| Let a martyrdom operation proceed | –1 (Sicarii term fulfilled) | Regional escalation feeds all | Moloch + Dagon fed |
| Call in an airstrike on a seal site | +2 (seal destroyed) | Accelerates everything | All three empowered |
| Repair a damaged seal | –2 (seal restored) | Stability returns | None — this is the heroic play |

### 3.4 The Order's Calculation

The Order of the Broken Sica maintains equilibrium by ensuring **no term is ever fully fulfilled or fully violated**. They:
- Kill extremists about to trigger mass martyrdom (preventing Sicarii fulfillment)
- Sabotage tunnel-deepening equipment (preventing Dagon fulfillment)
- Leak intel to prevent operations that would maximize youth casualties (preventing Moloch fulfillment)

But they also prevent peace — because if the war stops, the Pact destabilizes and the egregores notice the silence. Their ideal state: the Blood Engine idles at exactly 3 on the Pact Track.

### 3.5 The Fourth Path

The only way to escape the trilemma: **renegotiate the Pact**. This requires:
1. The War Scroll (located in the Shrine of the Book deep vault)
2. A reader capable of speaking the original covenant language
3. A sacrifice the egregores accept as replacement for the existing terms
4. The consent (or defeat) of all three factions

If the agents renegotiate successfully, they can replace the three terms with a new single term — one that doesn't feed on human suffering. The cost is whatever they offer in exchange. This is the heroic ending: not destroying the egregores, but *changing the contract*.

## 4. Factions

### Project Gibeon (Israeli Defense Apparatus)

- **Cover**: Deep-black psychological operations / antiquities unit
- **Belief**: The egregore is an automatic defense system. Its distortion field messes with enemy guidance, misdirects invaders. Without it, the state is vulnerable.
- **Goal**: Maintain the Blood Engine at current output. Keep the war hot enough to feed the entity, cold enough to avoid full destruction.
- **Trade-off**: Saving the shield means condemning the land to endless war.

#### Key NPC: Colonel (res.) Yael Talmor

**Role**: Director of Project Gibeon. Former Unit 8200 signals intelligence commander.

**Appearance**: Late 50s. Wire-framed glasses, grey-streaked hair pulled into a tight bun, always in civilian clothes that are one step too formal (blazer over a turtleneck, even in the field). Carries a well-worn leather satchel, not a weapon.

**Demeanor**: Pragmatic, exhausted, meticulously precise. She speaks in data — casualty statistics, signal-to-noise ratios, thermal anomaly graphs. She never raises her voice. This makes her more terrifying than any shouting commander.

**Belief**: She discovered the egregore's distortion field during a routine SIGINT analysis in 2018 — a persistent electromagnetic anomaly that correlated perfectly with periods of intense conflict. She spent years proving it was real, then years building Gibeon to manage it. She doesn't worship the egregore. She sees it as a shield, a horrible one, but one that has kept Israeli casualties statistically lower than models predict. She believes the war is terrible — and would be worse without her intervention.

**Connection to Character 1 (Tactical Lead)**: Yael personally recruited Character 1 into a "special SIGINT support unit" during their IDF service — a cover for an early Gibeon operation. The operation went wrong: three soldiers died, Character 1 was the sole survivor. Yael has always suspected Character 1 heard something in the radio static that night that they've never reported. She's right.

**Secret**: Yael knows the egregore is mutating. The distortion field is starting to *demand* specific sacrifices — she's received three "operational requirements" in the past year that, when carried out, resulted in exact numbers of casualties matching an ancient Near Eastern numerology pattern. She hasn't told her superiors. She's hoping the Agents can find another way before she has to follow through.

**What she wants from the Agents**: At first, she sees them as useful assets — deniable, foreign-affiliated, capable of operating where Gibeon can't. As the campaign progresses, she shifts to wanting them to either (a) validate her approach (so she's not acting alone) or (b) find a third option so she doesn't have to make the choice she's dreading.

**Handler note**: Yael is not a villain. She's a patriot who made a devil's bargain and is watching the interest compound. The Agents can potentially convert her as an ally if they demonstrate a viable alternative before she's forced to order the "nine casualty operation" (Moloch's numerology pattern suggests the next required sacrifice).

### The Keepers of the Well (Gaza Underground)

- **Cover**: Subterranean network of medics, engineers, elders — not tied to Hamas political goals
- **Belief**: Dagon absorbs and redistributes psychological weight. Without the entity as a sponge, the population would suffer collective psychotic break.
- **Goal**: Access the Shrine of the Book vaults to steal the War Scroll. Alter ritual geometry to expand Dagon's capacity.
- **Trade-off**: Expanding the entity mutates people into hollow, non-human things.

#### Key NPC: Dr. Mariam Abu Rashed

**Role**: Lead surgeon and de facto spiritual leader of the Keepers. Former head of trauma surgery at Al-Shifa Hospital.

**Appearance**: Mid-40s. Wears surgical scrubs under a heavy coat, even when not in the hospital. Deep circles under her eyes. Her hands are steady but her voice carries a permanent tremor of exhaustion. A small brass pendant — a stylized fish — hangs around her neck (a Philistine Dagon symbol she never removes).

**Demeanor**: Warm, clinical, profoundly sad. She treats everyone as a patient, even armed fighters. She listens more than she speaks. When she does speak, it's with the brutal honesty of someone who has seen too much to lie.

**Belief**: In 2023, during the worst period of Al-Shifa's operation, Mariam noticed a pattern: patients who should have broken — who had lost entire families — were *functional*. Not okay, but functional. The psychic weight was going somewhere. She traced it to the tunnels, to the deep places where Dagon stirs. She made contact. She didn't pray to Dagon — she *negotiated*. Dagon would absorb the grief, and in exchange, the Keepers would ensure the tunnels kept deepening. She believes she's saving her people's minds, one day at a time.

**Connection to Character 2 (Medic)**: Mariam was Character 2's attending physician during a medical mission to Gaza years ago — before the war escalated. She saw the potential in the younger doctor and pushed them toward trauma surgery. Character 2 may have idolized her. Now, Character 2 must confront what their mentor has become.

**Secret**: Mariam is beginning to mutate. It started in her hands — a slight webbing between her fingers that she hides with surgical gloves. She can hold her breath for seven minutes. At night, she dreams of a city at the bottom of the sea. She hasn't told anyone in the Keepers. She knows Dagon is changing her, and she's terrified — but she also knows the alternative is watching her people go mad in the streets.

**What she wants from the Agents**: Mariam wants confirmation that she's doing the right thing. She will defend the Keepers' work passionately — but if the Agents show her a true alternative to Dagon's bargain, she would take it. She wants to stop mutating. She wants to die human.

**Handler note**: Mariam is the most sympathetic faction leader. She's also the most dangerous — because she's desperate. A cornered Mariam will authorize the Keepers to accelerate their tunnel-deepening, triggering a major Pact increase, if she thinks Dagon is the only option left.

### The Order of the Broken Sica (Syncretic Watchers)

- **Cover**: Ancient lineage of custodians — Palestinian Christians, Druze, unorthodox Jewish mystics
- **Belief**: If you kill the egregores, the vacuum invites a greater Outer God to notice the empty space. They maintain equilibrium.
- **Goal**: Targeted assassinations and minor rituals to keep the conflict perfectly balanced. Neither side wins; peace never comes.
- **Trade-off**: They are actively opposing the agents. They present a terrifying argument: "You are trying to cure a fever by killing the patient."

#### Key NPC: Abuna Salim Jaber

**Role**: The current "Watchman" — the Order's operational leader in the West Bank and Jerusalem corridor. A Melkite Greek Catholic priest.

**Appearance**: Late 60s. Black priest's robe, grey beard, kind eyes that don't match the precision of his movements. He walks with a slight limp — an old knife wound from a Sicarii assassination in the 1980s. He carries a battered leather Bible that has the Order's secret texts inscribed in the margins in Aramaic.

**Demeanor**: Serene, patient, utterly immovable. He speaks in parables and questions. He never directly answers a request — he tells a story that contains the answer. He's been doing this for forty years and has developed the spiritual armor of someone who has made peace with terrible choices. He is not cruel. He is simply convinced he's right.

**Belief**: The Order inherited the truth from the original Pact signatories: the egregores are not the worst thing out there. They are territorial — limited to the Levant. The void they occupy is a *shield* against the attention of outer gods who have no geography and no limits. The war is terrible. But it is contained. Destroy the egregores, and the silence will be noticed. What answers the silence will be infinitely worse.

**Connection to Character 3 (Intelligence)**: Salim was a confidential informant of Character 3 during their Shin Bet service — "The Priest," a source who provided uncannily accurate intelligence about extremist plots. Character 3 always wondered how an elderly priest had access to that level of information. The answer: Salim was feeding Character 3 intel to maintain balance — preventing attacks that would trigger massive reprisals (and feed Moloch), while allowing attacks that were "contained enough" to maintain the Blood Engine's idle.

**Secret**: The Order has a contingency plan called "The Last Vigil" — a ritual that would kill every member of the Order simultaneously, creating a psychic shockwave that would seal the egregores for another 2000 years. The cost is every watcher's life. Salim carries the trigger — a small obsidian sica knife — at all times. If he believes the agents will succeed in breaking the Pact without a viable replacement, he will use it.

**What he wants from the Agents**: Salim initially wants to test them — to see if they're tools of another faction or genuinely independent. If they pass, he wants to recruit them as replacements. He's old. The Order needs new watchers. He sees the four dual-citizens as uniquely suited: they belong to both sides and neither, which is exactly the perspective the watchers need.

**Handler note**: Salim is the antagonist who might be right. His argument is compelling — and may *actually be true*. The Handler should seed evidence throughout the campaign that the Outer God threat is real (strange astronomical phenomena, dreams of vast empty spaces, Delta Green's own classified files about "The Silence"). This makes the climax choice genuinely agonizing: do the agents destroy the egregores and risk annihilation, or accept Salim's "eternal war" as the least-bad option?

## 7. Handler: Rivka Grossman

### Surface

**Age**: 68. Athletic build — swims daily, even in winter. Grey hair cut short and practical. Eyeglasses with a beaded band; the lenses have a faint amber tint. Dressed in clothes that could be civilian or military: dark trousers, a collared shirt, a jacket. No jewelry except a plain wedding band on her right hand.

The agents have never met each other in this configuration — only met Mrs. Grossman. She recruited each of them personally after their 1-on-1 contact with the Unnatural. They know her voice, her handwriting, her preferred meeting spots (cafés with good exits, museum lobbies, the back of a particular bookshop in West Jerusalem).

They do not know her first name. The letters are signed "— R. Grossman" but no one has ever called her anything but "Mrs. Grossman" to her face.

### The Full Truth (Handler's Eyes Only)

**1960-1973 — The Archaeologist**:
Rivka Grossman (an alias she adopted in the 1980s; her birth name is classified) was a promising young archaeologist at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem, specializing in Canaanite and Philistine material culture. She was present at the excavation of Tel Ashkelon, the Qumran caves survey, and several minor digs across the West Bank. She was 25, brilliant, and completely secular. She believed the ancient texts were just texts.

**October 1973 — The Golan Breach**:
During the Yom Kippur War, Rivka was embedded with an IDF antiquities salvage unit — a cover for a deeper intelligence operation tracking Syrian archaeology looted from border sites. A Syrian artillery shell hit a hillside in the Golan Heights, exposing a buried structure that matched no known building type. Rivka was the first to enter.

Inside: a chamber of basalt blocks, undamaged by the shelling. At its center, a stasis jar — identical to the one the agents find in Arc 2. The walls were covered in an Aramaic inscription that Rivka later learned was the first clause of the Pact. She touched it.

The vision lasted 47 seconds. She witnessed: the original Pact signing in a cave outside 1st-century Jerusalem, the faces of the signatories (a Judean priest, a Philistine merchant, a Hellenistic mystic), the binding of the three egregores, and a glimpse of what happens if the Pact ever fully breaks.

Three members of her team died that night. Not from the shell. They were found sitting against the chamber wall, eyes open, breathing, but entirely absent — catatonic, unresponsive, whispering Aramaic phrases that matched the inscription. Rivka survived because she was the only one who didn't try to run. She stood still. She listened.

**1974-1991 — Project Covenant**:
Rivka was recruited by a Delta Green predecessor — Project Covenant, a joint US-UK-Israeli cell that existed from 1948 to 1991. Her training officer was an American named Thomas Keane, who taught her everything she knows about the Unnatural. She was Covenant's only Israeli field agent.

Over 17 years, Rivka:
- Sealed a Dagon breach beneath the Gaza fishing port (1981)
- Recovered a fragment of the War Scroll from a Syrian antiquities dealer (1985)
- Killed a Moloch cultist who had infiltrated the Israeli Defense Ministry (1989)
- Watched Thomas Keane die in a tunnel under the Old City of Jerusalem, his throat cut by a Sicarii descendant (1991)

After Keane's death and Covenant's disbandment, Rivka went quiet. She maintained the network, kept the watch, but operated alone.

**The Glasses**:
The lenses are not glass. They're made from a crystalline mineral found in the Golan chamber — a byproduct of the Pact's binding energy, scavenged from the stasis jar's seal. The mineral is slightly piezoelectric: it resonates at the same frequency as the egregore field. Wearing them, Rivka can see the Unnatural as a visual distortion — heat shimmer, color shifts, shadows that slide a half-second behind their sources.

The beaded band is not decorative. Each bead is hand-carved from olive wood, each inscribed with a single character from the Binding Mark's protective variant. It keeps the glasses stable during physical activity and acts as a low-grade ward. Without them, Rivka can still see the Unnatural, but it overwhelms her — she takes 1 SAN per scene for every hour she goes without them.

**The Secret**:
Rivka is dying. The Pact contact in 1973 left a degenerative mark on her nervous system, dormant for decades and now accelerating. The official diagnosis is ALS. Rivka knows it's the Pact's accumulated interest on a debt she thought she'd paid in full.

She has 6-12 months. Maybe less.

These four agents are not her team. They are her legacy.

### Why Four Dual-Citizens

Rivka has been tracking each agent since their first contact with the Unnatural — in some cases, for years before she made contact.

She chose dual-citizens because people who belong entirely to one side cannot see the third side. The Israeli who cannot understand Palestinian grief cannot perceive Dagon's domain. The Palestinian who cannot understand Israeli fear cannot perceive Moloch's calculus. The agents' very existence — their dual identities, their divided loyalties, their ability to hold two truths at once — is what makes them capable of perceiving the full shape of the Pact.

She built the team deliberately to force them to depend on each other across political and cultural lines. The Group Bond is not an accident. It is Rivka's design.

### Relationships

**Yael Talmor (Project Gibeon)**:
Rivka recruited Yael into the intelligence community in the late 1990s. Yael was a young SIGINT analyst with an unusual gift for pattern recognition — she noticed the thermal anomaly that Rivka had been tracking for years. Rivka brought her into the circle, taught her about the egregore, and trusted her with the truth about the Pact.

Yael's founding of Project Gibeon was, from Rivka's perspective, a betrayal of that trust. Yael weaponized the knowledge — turned a containment operation into a manipulation operation. They have not spoken since 2022.

Rivka still believes Yael can be saved. She has not given up on her. This is the sentimental weakness that will either redeem Rivka or kill her.

**Mariam Abu Rashed (Keepers of the Well)**:
Rivka and Mariam met once, in 2019, at a medical conference in Cairo. Rivka was there investigating rumors of "unusual trauma patterns" in Gaza hospitals. Mariam was presenting a paper on war surgery. Rivka saw the signs in Mariam's data — the same pattern she recognized from the Golan in 1973, the same "psychic absorption" signature.

She tried to warn Mariam over coffee in the conference hotel. Mariam dismissed her as a conspiracy theorist with too much time and too little evidence. Rivka did not push. She has been monitoring Mariam ever since.

**Salim Jaber (Order of the Broken Sica)**:
Rivka and Salim have known each other for 31 years. They meet once a year at the same café in the Christian Quarter of Jerusalem's Old City — a tiny Armenian place called *Zalatimo's*. They order coffee and pastries. They talk about books, music, the weather in the Galilee. They do not discuss the Pact.

Salim knows Rivka is dying. He has offered her the Last Vigil — the clean death by the obsidian knife, on her own terms, at a time and place of her choosing. She has refused. Twice.

They have an unspoken agreement: if Rivka breaks before she dies — if the Pact or the egregores corrupt her final months — Salim will use the knife. She has given him permission.

### Handler Arc (Across the Campaign)

**Arcs 1-2**: Rivka is mission control — distant, professional, cryptic. She communicates by dead drop, encrypted text, brief phone calls. She is competent and intimidating. The agents should not know she is dying.

**Arc 3**: Her health declines. It starts small — a forgotten detail in a briefing, a slight tremor in her handwriting on a dead-drop note. By the end of the arc, she cancels a meeting. She never cancels meetings. The agents may begin to suspect something is wrong.

**Arc 4 (before the climax)**: Rivka appears in person at the Shrine of the Book. This is unexpected — she has never come to the field before. She looks thinner. Tired. She gives the agents her glasses before the descent: *"I won't need them where I'm going."* She does not explain.

**Climax**: Rivka is present in the warped Shrine. She can help — or she can close the door behind the agents, sealing them inside with the factions. Her final choice determines whether the agents face the climax alone or with her support.

### Ending Possibilities

| Outcome | Rivka's Fate |
|---------|-------------|
| **Fourth Path** | The Pact renegotiation heals the degenerative mark. Rivka lives — perhaps for the first time in 50 years, she is not bound to the region. She leaves. The agents inherit her network. |
| **Project Gibeon wins** | Rivka dies — either by Yael's hand (a mercy killing, Yael's last act of love for her teacher) or by her own hand, refusing to serve the machine she helped create. |
| **Keepers win** | Rivka is lost in the Shrine collapse. Her body is never found. The agents find her glasses among the rubble, cracked. |
| **Order wins (Last Vigil)** | Salim triggers the Last Vigil. Rivka is among the 47 watchers who die. It is what she wanted — a meaningful death, on her own terms. The agents find her body in the underground vault, sitting against the black basalt wall, her eyes closed, her glasses folded beside her. |
| **Pact Breach** | Rivka is the first to see the egregore manifest. She faces it, alone, buying the agents time to escape. The last thing they hear is her voice on the radio: *"Run. I'll hold the door."* |

### Mechanical Notes

- **If Rivka gives the agents her glasses**: The wearer can see the Unnatural (automatic success on Occult rolls related to detecting egregore influence). However, wearing them too long (more than one scene per session) causes 1 SAN loss — the wearer begins to see *too much*.
- **If Rivka dies before the climax**: The agents lose access to Delta Green support. No more resupply, no more intelligence, no more cover stories. They must rely entirely on each other. The Group Bond becomes even more important.
- **If Rivka survives the campaign**: She becomes an ally for future campaigns. The agents gain access to her network — a small but powerful asset.

## 9. The Pact Mechanics

### 8.1 Overview

Two player-facing tracks that underpin the entire campaign. The Pact Track (cosmic) and the Static Die (mundane). They interact: a high Pact Level makes the Static Die tick up faster.

Both are visible to the players — written on a whiteboard, pinned in the Discord channel, or printed on the table handout. The horror comes from watching them climb.

### 8.2 The Pact Track (0–10)

| Level | State | World Effects |
|-------|-------|---------------|
| 0 | **Dormant** | Egregores silent. Unnatural effects impossible. Peace is structurally possible. |
| 1–2 | **Quiet** | Static on radios. Strange dreams. The egregores are aware but patient. |
| 3–4 | **Murmur** | Campaign start zone. Coincidences accumulate. Electronics glitch near seal sites. |
| 5–6 | **Pressing** | Reality warps locally. Tunnels remember older shapes. Objects move. Static Die increments doubled. |
| 7–8 | **Manifestation** | Egregores project avatars. The War Scroll resonates. Buildings remember their older, stranger foundations. |
| 9 | **Threshold** | Seals cracking. The egregores press against reality. Visible anomalies to anyone. |
| 10 | **Breach** | Egregores physically enter. Campaign endgame — possibly a TPK or world-altering conclusion. |

**Starting Level**: 3 — the seals are damaged but holding. The war is the equilibrium.

### 8.3 Spending the Pact (Invoking the Debt)

Players may voluntarily increase the Pact Level to gain unnatural effects. This represents calling on the egregores' power — accepting a debt that tightens their grip on reality.

| Cost | Effect Type | Examples |
|------|-------------|----------|
| +1 | **Minor Twist** | Reroll a failed skill check. A door sticks. A light flickers at the right moment. |
| +2 | **Lesser Boon** | Speak a language you don't know. Extinguish all flames in a room. Cause overwhelming unease in one target. Read a surface thought. |
| +3 | **Major Working** | Animate an object. Inflict a SAN-damaging vision. Briefly turn invisible to one observer. Seal a door unnaturally. |
| +4 | **Egregore Bargain** | The entity takes interest. Direct intervention: a tunnel collapses on command, a soldier freezes in terror, the air itself becomes solid. The Handler chooses the cost — it always involves a Pact Term. |

**Rule**: The player describes what they want. The Handler names the Pact Level increase. The player accepts or refuses. No takebacks.

**Pact debt can be invoked even without an artifact or ritual** — the egregores are always listening. But using it near a seal site, during a ritual, or while observed multiplies the Static Die increase.

### 8.4 What Moves the Pact Track

**Increases (+)**:
| Event | Amount |
|-------|--------|
| A seal is destroyed | +2 |
| Mass casualty event in a conflict zone | +1 |
| A ritual is performed incorrectly | +1 |
| A Pact Term is violated | +1–3 |
| A player spends the Pact (see 8.3) | varies |
| An egregore avatar manifests | +2 |

**Decreases (–)**:
| Event | Amount |
|-------|--------|
| A seal is repaired or reinforced | –2 |
| Innocent lives saved through direct intervention | –1 |
| A correct ritual is performed | –1 |
| A genuine personal sacrifice (not HP, but a Bond, a possession, a memory) | –1 to –3 |
| A Pact Term the egregores *want* fulfilled is fulfilled (they honour the contract) | –1 |
| A faction is neutralized or converted | –1 |

**Campaign events** (scripted):
- End of Arc 1: +1 (the agents understand the Pact but have disturbed the first seal)
- End of Arc 2: +2 (the Baal temple breach accelerates everything)
- End of Arc 3: +1 (Dagon's attention is now focused on the agents)

### 8.5 The Static Die (d10)

A d10 tracking the mundane world's attention on the agents. It represents surveillance, witnesses, blown cover, physical evidence.

**Starting value**: 1

**Increases** (roll +1 each time, unless noted):
- Leaving physical evidence at a scene
- Killing in view of witnesses
- Using the Pact (see 8.3) in a populated area: +2
- Failing a cover identity check: +1
- An ally or asset is compromised: +2
- High Pact Level (5+): all Static increases are +1 higher

**Decreases**:
- Burning a cover identity: –2
- Laying low for a full week in-game: –1
- Successful disinformation operation: –2
- Paying off or eliminating a witness: –1

**On 10**: The heat crystallizes. Roll d4:
1. A tail — Shin Bet, Mossad, or a faction spots them.
2. Evidence — their fingerprints, photo, or alias appears in an investigation.
3. A leak — their cover story has a hole. Someone they spoke to reports them.
4. Direct contact — a faction operative approaches them. Hostile or curious.

After the complication, the Static Die resets to 5 (the heat doesn't disappear; it recedes).

### 8.6 Track Interaction

| Pact Level | Static Die Behaviour |
|------------|----------------------|
| 0–2 | Static Die ticks down 1 extra step during downtime (cosmic calm aids obscurity) |
| 3–4 | Normal behaviour |
| 5–6 | All Static increases are +1 higher |
| 7–8 | Static Die cannot drop below 4. The cosmic pressure leaks into mundane attention. |
| 9–10 | Static Die ticks up 1 automatically at session start. Reality itself is noticing. |

## 9. Pact Artifacts Catalog

Artifacts the agents can acquire across the campaign. Each has a function and a cost. Most are reusable but accumulate Pact Debt over time.

### 9.1 Brass Fish Pendant (Dagon)

**First appearance**: Character 1's 1-on-1 (Gaza tunnel altar). Also available in Arc 3 (Non-Euclidean Network).

**Description**: A cast-brass pendant in the shape of a stylized fish, Philistine period (c. 1000 BCE). Warm to the touch even in cold air. When held underwater, it emits a faint bioluminescent glow.

**Function**:
- Passive: The wearer can sense the nearest underground water source within 1km.
- Active (spend 1 WP, Pact +1): The wearer can perceive all living creatures within 50m through solid earth — heartbeats, breathing, movement. Lasts one scene.
- Active (spend 2 WP, Pact +2): The wearer can breathe underwater for one hour.

**Cost**: Each use raises Pact Level. Wearing it for extended periods causes dreams of a city at the bottom of the sea. After 3 sessions of wear, the character must make a SAN roll (0/1d4) or become convinced the dreams are real memories.

**Handler note**: The pendant is Dagon's anchor in the surface world. She can speak through it. Characters who wear it are easier for her to influence. At key moments (the climax), Dagon may issue a command through the pendant — the player can resist with a CHAx5 roll or unconsciously comply.

---

### 9.2 Obsidian Sica Knife (Sicarii / Order)

**First appearance**: Arc 1 (carried by Salim Jaber) or Arc 4 (discovered in the Order's cache under the Shrine).

**Description**: A curved dagger of black obsidian, Roman-era, 20cm blade. The handle is wrapped in aged leather that smells of myrrh and old blood. The blade has never dulled. It reflects light imperfectly — shadows in the reflection are longer than they should be.

**Function**:
- Active (Pact +1, SAN 1/1d4): Draw blood with the knife. The victim experiences a vision of the First-Century Pact signing. This can be used as an interrogation tool or to reveal campaign lore.
- Active (Pact +3): The "Last Vigil" ritual. Plunging the knife into one's own throat in a specific geographic location triggers a psychic shockwave that seals the egregores for 2000 years. Kills the user instantly. Irreversible.

**Cost**: Obsidian is brittle. A fumbled roll (01-03 on d%) shatters the blade, destroying the artifact. The Order has only two remaining sica knives.

**Handler note**: If the agents acquire this, Salim will pursue them relentlessly — not out of anger, but because he must recover it to prevent misuse or to use it himself if the agents break the Pact. The Last Vigil is a campaign-ending event. If the agents trigger it, the campaign ends with the Order's sacrifice and the egregores sealed. Roll credits.

---

### 9.3 Bronze Shekel of Ba'al (Moloch)

**First appearance**: Arc 2 (Ashlar Bastion — found in the stasis jar within the Baal temple).

**Description**: A coin of bronze and lead alloy, the size of a modern shekel. Stamped on one side: the profile of Ba'al-Hammon seated on a throne, a child on his lap. On the reverse: a flame.

**Function**:
- Active (Pact +1 per use): "The Accountant's Eye." Hold the coin to your eye and look at a person. You see their "value" as a flame — a numerical value representing their contribution to the Blood Engine (how much suffering their death or survival would generate). Higher flame = more Pact significance.
- Active (Pact +2): "The Ledger." Press the coin to a wound and speak a name. The wound transfers to the named person (if within 1km). The original wound closes. The target must make a CON save or take the damage.
- Passive: Anyone carrying the coin for more than a week begins to unconsciously optimize their decisions for maximum casualty "efficiency." The Handler should occasionally offer the player a +20% bonus to a tactical roll, then reveal the plan caused collateral damage.

**Cost**: The coin is addictive. The more the agents use it, the more Moloch's logic infects them — seeing people as numbers, sacrifices as investments. Each use beyond the first requires a SAN roll (0/1d3) or the character argues for the "efficient" option in the next decision.

**Handler note**: The coin is Moloch's ledger. If the agents use it extensively, introduce an NPC they care about, then have the coin show that NPC's death would be "optimal" for the Pact. See if they make the trade.

---

### 9.4 The Copper Scroll

**First appearance**: Arc 4 climax (Shrine of the Book deep vault).

**Description**: A corroded copper scroll, identical to the real Copper Scroll of Qumran (3Q15). When fully unrolled, it measures 2.4m. The text is 1st-century Hebrew mixed with cryptographic symbols — ancient Hebrew, Aramaic, and Greek letter substitutions. The text shifts and rewrites itself based on the reader's intention.

**Function**:
- The scroll is the original Pact document. Reading it aloud in the original Hebrew binds the reader and all present to the Pact's terms.
- Reading it *as an oath* (Pact +5): The reader becomes a new Pact signatory. The Pact Level immediately drops to 2. The egregores are re-bound for another generation. The reader gains a permanent Unnatural score of 50% and loses 1d10 CHA (they are no longer fully human — they are now a party to the contract).
- Reading it *as a revision* (requires the War Scroll and all three keys): The text can be edited. The reader can rewrite one Pact Term. This is the Fourth Path.

**Cost**: Attempting to read the scroll without speaking the correct ritual language triggers a Pact Level increase of +3 and summons an egregore manifestation (combat encounter).

**Handler note**: The Copper Scroll should feel like a nuclear launch code. The agents find it late in the campaign. It's not a tool — it's a responsibility. Give the players time to read the text, discuss it, and understand what they're about to do. A hasty reading should have consequences.

---

### 9.5 The War Scroll (1QM — Tuning Fork)

**First appearance**: Arc 4 climax (Shrine of the Book deep vault, behind the black basalt wall).

**Description**: Not a scroll at all. A crystal rod, 30cm long, inscribed with the same text as the Dead Sea War Scroll (1QM). The crystal vibrates at a frequency that resonates with human bone. Holding it causes a deep sense of *correctness* — as if the universe briefly aligns.

**Function**:
- The War Scroll is a *tuning fork for reality*. It can adjust the resonant frequency of the regional egregore field.
- When placed in the central socket of the Shrine of the Book (under the white dome), it can:
  - **Amplify** the egregores (Pact Level +3, empowers one faction)
  - **Dampen** the egregores (Pact Level -2, weakens all factions)
  - **Reprogram** the Pact (requires the Copper Scroll, all three keys, and a reader)

**Cost**: The War Scroll cannot be used safely without understanding. Each use without three successful Occult/Unnatural rolls causes:
- 1d6 SAN loss (the tuning vibrates the reader's soul)
- +1 Pact Level (the frequency bleeds)
- A localized reality glitch (all electronics within 50m fail for 1d4 hours)

**Handler note**: The War Scroll is the climax centerpiece. The agents must choose *what to do with it*. Each faction wants it for a different purpose. The scroll's true power is only revealed when combined with the Copper Scroll and the three keys — this is the Fourth Path, and the agents should discover this through research and NPC interaction, not by accident.

---

### 9.6 The Binding Mark (Knowledge)

**First appearance**: Character 2's 1-on-1 (the patient's chest cavity) and recurring throughout.

**Description**: A geometric pattern — a hexagram enclosing a stylized fish. It appears in the tunnels, on the War Scroll, carved into the Shrine's foundations, and in the Dagon-warped architecture of Gaza's deep tunnels.

**Function**:
- Knowledge artifact. Cannot be held, but can be reproduced.
- Copying the mark onto a surface creates a *Dagon-safe zone* — within 5m, Dagon's influence is dampened. Unnatural effects originating from Dagon take -20% to affect anyone in the zone.
- The mark can be used to *bind* a Dagon-corrupted creature, immobilizing it for 1d4 rounds (requires a successful Unnatural roll and one full action).
- The mark's opposite (an inverted hexagram over an empty fish outline) is a *Dagon beacon* — it attracts the Deep Terror's attention.

**Cost**: Drawing the mark requires 1 WP and leaves a visible trace (burned into stone, bleeding from a finger, etc.). The inverted version requires Pact +1 each time.

**Handler note**: The Binding Mark is the agents' best defense against Dagon's influence. They should find it early (Arc 1 caves) and its full meaning should unfold. By Arc 3, drawing it should be reflexive. By the climax, they'll need it to navigate the warped Shrine.

---

### 9.7 The Aleppo Codex Pages (Fragmentary)

**First appearance**: Arc 1 (Nablus cave ossuary).

**Description**: Six parchment fragments from the Aleppo Codex, the oldest and most authoritative Hebrew Bible. The canonical text is familiar, but *between* the lines, in a script visible only under ultraviolet light, are geographic annotations: coordinates, depth measurements, seal locations across Gaza and Syria.

**Function**:
- Lore artifact. The fragments are a map of the seal network.
- When all six are assembled and correctly ordered, they reveal the location of:
  1. The Baal temple (Arc 2)
  2. The Dagon convergence point beneath Gaza (Arc 3)
  3. The access shaft to the Shrine's deep vault (Arc 4)
- Each fragment also contains one "Pact Law" — a rule of the original contract. Knowing these laws gives the agents +20% on Occult rolls related to the Pact.

**Cost**: The fragments are fragile. Handling them without proper equipment causes 1d4% structural damage per scene. At 0%, the fragment turns to dust (permanent loss). The agents must choose: study them carefully (safe) or move fast (risky).

**Handler note**: These are the campaign's "map." Without them, the agents are flying blind. If the players lose or destroy a fragment, they can still proceed — but they'll take a +1 Pact Level penalty (they're operating without full knowledge) and miss key clues.

### 9.9 Three Keys of the Fourth Path

To renegotiate the Pact, the agents must gather three keys:

1. **The Fish** — the Brass Fish Pendant (Dagon's mark / the agents' passage through the deep places)
2. **The Bronze** — the Bronze Shekel of Ba'al (Moloch's ledger / the agents' understanding of the economy of sacrifice)
3. **The Key in the Heart of the Black Wall** — a void-core crystal embedded in the Shrine of the Book's black basalt wall (the Sicarii egregore's prison-lock / the Order's burden)

The War Scroll inserts into the Shrine's central socket. The Three Keys slot into three recesses on the scroll's base. The Copper Scroll is placed before it. A reader speaks the revision.

Without all three keys, the Fourth Path is impossible. Each key corresponds to one egregore's acceptance of the new terms — the agents must have *earned* the right to speak for each entity. This happens through fulfilling or challenging each egregore's domain across the campaign's arcs.

## 11. Campaign Failure Timeline

Delta Green campaigns shouldn't end when the agents fail — they should get *worse*. Each arc has escalating failure states that narrow the options for the climax. The campaign remains winnable (and meaningful) even after multiple failures.

### 11.1 Arc 1: "The Silent Vigil" — West Bank

**Required success condition**: Retrieve at least 3 of the 6 Aleppo Codex fragments. Learn the Binding Mark.

| Outcome | Pact Δ | Consequences |
|---------|--------|-------------|
| **Complete success** (all 6 fragments + Binding Mark) | +0 | Full seal map available. Arc 2-3 locations revealed. +20% Occult from Pact Laws. |
| **Partial success** (3-5 fragments) | +1 | Partial map. Arc 2 location known, Arc 3 location unclear. No Pact Laws. |
| **Minimum success** (1-2 fragments + Binding Mark) | +2 | Agents know something is in Syria and Gaza but not where. Must find Arc 2 through other means. |
| **Failure** (no fragments, but saw the cave) | +3 | Agents know the Pact exists but have no map. Project Gibeon or the Order finds the fragments instead. |
| **Total failure** (captured, artifacts destroyed) | +4 | The fragments are lost or seized by Shin Bet. The agents are burned from the start. Static Die starts at 4 instead of 1. |

**Escalation**: If the agents fail, Yael Talmor's team recovers the fragments. She knows what the agents are now. Project Gibeon has a head start on tracking them.

### 11.2 Arc 2: "The Ashlar Bastion" — Syria/Golan

**Required success condition**: Retrieve the Bronze Shekel from the Baal temple.

| Outcome | Pact Δ | Consequences |
|---------|--------|-------------|
| **Complete success** (Shekel retrieved, no faction casualties) | +1 | Fourth Path remains available. The agents have one of the Three Keys. |
| **Partial success** (Shekel retrieved, but faction casualties or identification) | +2 | Shekel acquired, but the agents are now wanted by one faction. That faction sends hunters in Arc 3. |
| **Failure** (Shekel not retrieved, but agents escape) | +3 | The Shekel is lost in the Baal temple rubble. The Fourth Path is **locked**. Agents must choose a faction ending in the climax. |
| **Total failure** (agents captured or the Shekel is taken by a faction) | +4 | A faction now has the Shekel. They understand the agents' mission. The Pact accelerates. The agents must escape captivity during Arc 3. |

**Fourth Path lock**: If the Bronze Shekel is lost, the agents cannot renegotiate the Pact. The climax becomes a choice between the three faction outcomes. This should feel like a real loss — remind the players of the Bronze Shekel's description from Character 4's 1-on-1 fragment ("bring the bronze").

### 11.3 Arc 3: "The Non-Euclidean Network" — Gaza

**Required success condition**: Map the Dagon convergence point. Learn the truth about the Keepers. Character 4's family is safe.

| Outcome | Pact Δ | Consequences |
|---------|--------|-------------|
| **Complete success** (tunnels mapped, Keepers understood, family safe) | +1 | The agents know the Shrine's deep vault access point. Mariam is a potential ally. Fish pendant acquired (if not already). |
| **Partial success** (tunnels mapped, but family captured or Keepers hostile) | +2 | The agents have the map but Mariam is now an enemy. She knows their faces. The family becomes leverage in the climax. |
| **Failure** (tunnels not mapped, agents escape wounded) | +3 | The agents enter the climax without knowing the Shrine's underground access. They must find an above-ground route. The Pact is high. |
| **Total failure** (agents captured by Keepers or lost in the deep) | +4 | Dagon claims the agents. They escape (or are released) transformed — permanent SAN loss, a physical mutation, and a Pact Debt that the egregore can call in during the climax. |

**Family leverage**: If Character 4's family is captured, they become a bargaining chip at the Shrine. A faction (likely Project Gibeon or the Keepers) uses them to force Character 4's cooperation. This triggers a Group Bond test.

### 11.4 Climax: "The Light and The Dark" — Jerusalem

The climax doesn't have "failure" — it has **outcomes**. Every outcome is the end of the campaign.

| Outcome | Pact Δ | World State |
|---------|--------|-------------|
| **Project Gibeon wins** | Stabilizes at 4 | The war continues. The egregore remains as a national shield. The agents are absorbed into Gibeon — or silenced. Yael gets what she wanted, but Moloch's numerology pattern tightens. A year later, the "nine casualty" operation becomes the "ninety casualty" operation. |
| **Keepers of the Well win** | Stabilizes at 6 | Dagon expands. Gaza's tunnels deepen beyond mapping. The coastal population begins slow mutation — gills, scales, a new language. Mariam becomes something other than human. The war stops because the people are no longer fully people. |
| **Order of the Broken Sica wins** | Drops to 1 | The Last Vigil is triggered. All 47 watchers die. The egregores are sealed for another 2000 years. The war continues its normal course — without supernatural influence. Salim dies with the obsidian knife in his throat, satisfied. The agents inherit the vigil — or walk away, knowing the Order died for their choice. |
| **Fourth Path (renegotiation)** | Set to the new terms | The Pact is rewritten. The agents choose what the new term is — and what they sacrifice in exchange. The land settles. The war continues (it's still geopolitics) but the supernatural engine is gone. New term example: "The first-born of every generation shall live." Or: "What lies beneath shall remain below." Or: "The watchers shall rest." |
| **Pact Breach (agents fail completely)** | Hits 10 | The egregores manifest physically. Moloch walks as a pillar of burning ash. Dagon rises from the coast, the water turning to blood. The Sicarii egregore spreads as a psychic plague of apocalyptic fervor. The campaign ends with a world-altering catastrophe. The agents are the first casualties — or the last witnesses. |

### 11.5 Failure Escalation Summary

```
ARC 1 FAILURE → No seal map, Project Gibeon is alerted, Static starts high
       │
       ▼
ARC 2 FAILURE → Fourth Path locked (no Bronze Shekel), faction hunters, Pact accelerates
       │
       ▼
ARC 3 FAILURE → No underground access to Shrine, Mariam is enemy, family is leverage
       │
       ▼
ARC 4 → Fewer options, higher Pact, more enemies in the building
        The Fourth Path may already be impossible
```

The campaign stays playable through any combination of failures — but each failure narrows the climax toward one of the faction endings. Only by succeeding at all three arcs can the agents reach the Fourth Path.

### 11.6 The "Borrowed Time" Mechanic

If the Pact Level ever hits 9 before the climax, the agents are on borrowed time. Each session after reaching 9:
- Static Die ticks up +1 at session start (cannot be reduced below 6)
- All Pact spending costs +1 extra Level
- The Handler picks one environment change per scene (reality is degrading)
- If Pact hits 10, the Breach ending triggers immediately, even if the agents haven't completed all arcs

This creates pressure: the agents cannot delay forever. They must push forward, even when they're not ready.
