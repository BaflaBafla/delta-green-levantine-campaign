Here are a few ideas for archaeological locations in Israel and the surrounding region that could be tied to ancient cults and rituals, suitable for a Delta Green campaign:
1. Ba'albek, Lebanon:
    • Ancient God: Ba'al, the Canaanite god of storms and fertility.
    • Archaeological Significance: The massive Roman temple complex at Ba'albek was built on the site of an even older temple dedicated to Ba'al. The site contains massive stone blocks, some of the largest ever quarried, suggesting a connection to ancient, powerful rituals.
    • Potential Rituals: Human sacrifice, blood rituals, and the invocation of storms and earthquakes to demonstrate the power of Ba'al.
2. Qumran Caves, Israel:
    • Ancient God: Possibly a syncretic deity combining elements of various ancient Near Eastern religions, including the Dead Sea Scrolls' enigmatic "Teacher of Righteousness."
    • Archaeological Significance: The Qumran Caves, home to the Dead Sea Scrolls, offer a glimpse into a secretive sect that practiced unique rituals and beliefs.
    • Potential Rituals: Esoteric studies, purification rites, and the anticipation of a cosmic apocalypse.
3. Tel Megiddo, Israel:
    • Ancient God: Possibly associated with various deities, including the Canaanite god of war, Resheph.
    • Archaeological Significance: Tel Megiddo, the site of the biblical Armageddon, has a long history of warfare and religious significance.
    • Potential Rituals: Ritual sacrifices to ensure victory in battle, divination, and the invocation of destructive forces.
4. Petra, Jordan:
    • Ancient God: Dushara, the Nabatean god of fortune and prosperity.
    • Archaeological Significance: The stunning rock-cut city of Petra was a major religious center for the Nabateans.
    • Potential Rituals: Incense offerings, animal sacrifices, and rituals to ensure the flow of wealth and good fortune.
5. Göbekli Tepe, Turkey:
    • Ancient God: Unknown, but possibly a pre-historic deity associated with fertility and the hunt.
    • Archaeological Significance: Göbekli Tepe is one of the world's oldest known temples, predating many other ancient sites.
    • Potential Rituals: Ritual feasts, shamanic practices, and the worship of animal spirits.
